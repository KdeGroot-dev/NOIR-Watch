The Atelier

Crafted by
human hands.

In our Geneva atelier, every movement is assembled by a single master watchmaker from first component to final regulation. No assembly line. No shortcuts. Each piece bears the signature of its creator — a tradition unchanged since 1887.

01

Movement Design

Each calibre is conceived in-house, drawn by hand before a single component is machined.

02

Hand Finishing

Geneva stripes, bevelling, and mirror polishing — applied under 40× magnification.

03

Regulation

Each movement is tested across six positions for a minimum of 15 days before casing.

04

Certification

Every NOIR timepiece carries the Geneva Seal — the highest mark in watchmaking.

Since 1887

A century of
silent mastery.

NOIR was founded in Geneva in 1887 by Édouard Renard, a watchmaker who believed that the most profound luxury is invisible — felt only by those who understand what they hold.

"A watch does not tell you what time it is. It tells you who you are."

— Édouard Renard, founder, 1887

Five generations later, every decision at NOIR is guided by a single question: will this still matter in a hundred years?
